`uninhabited` classifies *entries* that reserve or define design space without local inhabitants.

An uninhabited entry may name a category, relation, extension point, or conceptual region
that the project wants to keep visible before concrete entries or implementation exist.

Use `uninhabited` when the absence of instances is intentional and useful to know.
Remove it when the design space gains ordinary local inhabitants.
